Associate Professor

Saint Louis University

I am an associate professor in the Department of Pharmacology and Physiology at Saint Louis University. My research focuses on transcription (the process of transcribing the genetic information from DNA to RNA). Transcription is regulated by multiple rate-limiting steps. My research focuses on the early steps in the recruitment of transcription factors and coregulators (coactivators and corepressors). For coregulators, we study scaffold proteins as well as enzymes such as histone deacetylases (HDACs) and histone acetyltransferases (HATs).

Our research uses complementary, interdisciplinary tools, including reductive and systems approaches, such as assays to measure protein-protein and protein-DNA interactions, assays to measure the transcription of model genes, the study of structure-function relationships by mutagenesis and molecular dynamics simulation, large-scale analysis of chromatin recruitment (ChIP-Seq), global (RNA-Seq) and singe-cells (scRNA-Seq) transcription, and modeling the relationship between gene expression and disease progression by analyzing patient samples using the TCGA and TARGET datasets. My CV can be downloaded from here.

I also serve as one of the academic editors for PLOS ONE.
