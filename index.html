Welcome

I am an associate professor in the Department of Pharmacology and Physiology at Saint Louis University and a member of the Alvin J. Siteman Cancer Center at the Barnes-Jewish Hospital and Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis. My research focuses on transcription (the process of transcribing the genetic information from DNA to RNA). Transcription is regulated by different rate-limiting steps. We focus on the initial steps in the recruitment of sequence-specific transcription factors and the subsequent recruitment and function of coregulators (coactivators and corepressors) that do the actual work in regulating transcription. We study both scaffold and enzymatic coregulators. The latter include histone deacetylases (HDACs) and histone acetyltransferases (HATs).

Our research uses interdisciplinary tools encompassing both reductive and systems approaches, such as assays for measuring protein-protein and protein-DNA interactions, assays for measuring transcription rates of model and natural genes, structure-functional studies by mutagenesis and molecular dynamics simulations, genome-wide analysis of chromatin occupancy (ChIP-Seq) and gene transcription at the global (RNA-Seq) and single-cell levels (scRNA-Seq), mouse knockouts, modeling disease progression using patient-derived xenografts, and analysis of patient samples, including those available in the TCGA and TARGET datasets.
